# Vannevar Bush

American engineer, inventor, and science administrator. Directed the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development during WWII. Best known in the knowledge-management lineage for his 1945 *Atlantic Monthly* essay ["As We May Think"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/As_We_May_Think), which introduced the [[Memex]] — a conceptual personal knowledge store with associative trails between documents.

## Relevance to this wiki

Karpathy's [[LLM Wiki Pattern]] explicitly positions itself as a Memex descendant. The gist's framing:

> "The idea relates in spirit to Vannevar Bush's Memex (1945) — a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents. Bush's vision was closer to this than what the web became: private, actively curated, with connections between documents as valuable as documents themselves. The part he couldn't solve was maintenance. The LLM handles that."

Bush's core intuition — that associations between documents carry as much value as the documents themselves — anticipates wiki-style cross-references by decades. What Bush couldn't automate (the bookkeeping) is precisely what LLMs now absorb.
